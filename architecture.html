BOISE REFUGE CENTER

1st Place ICMA Competition Winner

Boise, ID Refugee/Culture Center

 

Much like the way stained glass windows in churches tell stories, the secondary facade acts as a story telling interface from the building to the public.

 

Most of the refugees are traumatized from the fact they had to leave their home country and enter a whole new country which have different values, ways  of living, and most importantly, a different language. Instead of trying to identify a usual American building labeled with English logos on their first trip, they are confronted with a building that brings a sense of belonging and they immediately know this will be their new “rock”. The tinted glass panels of men, women, old, young of various types of people that wrap the second floor of the building greet the refugees and give a sense of not being alone.

 

During annual events, the panels can be take fitted with a story about the up coming event and would invite the local area to come meet the refugees and learn about their culture, through fresh organic food they grew, custom-made crafts, performance or other activities. This would allow the refugees to network with the local community and feel that they are welcomed into Boise. The more welcome they feel as a part of the new community, the more they will want to contribute to their new society.

TECHNICAL DRAWINGS

 

My first semester in college was spent learning how to do technically drawings using analog techniques. Spaces were defined using line-weights rather than simply poche. I take pride in accomplishing even the finest of details on a drawing.

 

I also taught myself how to draw in 3-point perspective as well as finding the shadow of an object from different sun directions.
